## About ICTAM

**ICT Association of Malawi (ICTAM)** is an umbrella body of all ICT professionals in Malawi. Our mission is to offer ICT leadership by catalyzing policy changes and supporting related developments aimed at enabling Malawians to participate effectively in the modern technology-based global economy, benefiting the Nation and its partners.

### Vision
To develop and promote the integrity and competence of the Information Technology (IT) profession in Malawi.

### Mission
To develop and promote the integrity and competence of the Information Technology (IT) profession in Malawi.

### Code of Conduct
Members of the ICT Association of Malawi will conduct their business in a professional way; will maintain ethical standards of conduct and at all times deal fairly and honestly with each other, clients, employees, suppliers and with the public.

## Logo Concept

The ICTAM logo consists of three key elements that combine to create a powerful symbol:

### Elements Breakdown

1. **Abstract Map of Malawi** (Gray silhouette)
   - Represents the country of Malawi
   - Geographic identity and national presence

2. **Connected Dots & Nodes** (Red circles of varying sizes)
   - Represent digital communication channels
   - Symbolize network connectivity
   - Show the interconnected nature of ICT

3. **Central Hub** (Large circle with ring)
   - Represents the central hub connected to other entities
   - Center of research and all ICT information
   - ICTAM's role as the central coordinating body

**Formula:** Abstract Map + Digital Communication Nodes + Central Hub = ICTAM Main Icon

### Symbolic Meaning
The logo visually communicates:
- **Connectivity**: The dots and lines show digital networks
- **Centrality**: ICTAM as the central hub for ICT in Malawi
- **Growth**: Varying sizes of circles suggest expansion and development
- **Unity**: All elements connected, representing collaboration

## Logo Formats

### Primary Logo (Horizontal)
The horizontal ICTAM logo is the **preferred (primary)** version and should be used on all applications where space allows.

**Components:**
- Icon on the left
- "ICTAM" wordmark in black (Gotham Bold)
- "ICT ASSOCIATION OF MALAWI" tagline in gray (Gotham Book)

### Vertical Logo
Use the vertical version when:
- Space calls for a vertical treatment
- Horizontal space is limited
- Square or portrait formats are required

### Secondary Variations
1. **Short Form**: Icon + "ICTAM" wordmark only
2. **Icon Only**: Standalone map symbol (use only as supplement to main logo)
3. **Monochrome**: Black or white versions for specific applications

**Important:** Never use the ICTAM logotype as a standalone graphic element without the icon.

## Logo Sizes

### Print Minimum Sizes
- **Horizontal with tagline**: 40mm minimum height
- **Horizontal without tagline**: 30mm minimum height
- **Vertical with tagline**: 15mm minimum height
- **Icon only**: 10mm minimum height

### Digital Minimum Sizes
- **Horizontal with tagline**: 150px minimum height
- **Horizontal without tagline**: 100px minimum height
- **Vertical with tagline**: 60px minimum height
- **Icon only**: 40px minimum height

## Construction & Boundaries

### Clear Space
It is vital that the ICTAM logo has adequate space around it to keep its message clear.

**Measurement:** The margin is defined by the **"M"** height of the selected logo variation.

**Clear Space Rules:**
- Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of "M" on all sides
- No text, graphics, or other elements should enter this clear space
- Applies to both horizontal and vertical formats

## Backgrounds

### Preferred Usage
- **Full color logo** on **white background** (ideal)
- Solid backgrounds with sufficient contrast
- Avoid busy photography or patterned backgrounds

### Alternative Versions
- **White logo** on dark backgrounds (black, red, dark gray)
- **Black logo** on light backgrounds
- Use monochromatic versions only when absolutely necessary

### Contrast Requirements
Always ensure sufficient contrast between logo and background for legibility.

---

## Logo Misuse

### DO NOT:

1. **Recreate the logo in any form**
   - Never attempt to redraw or recreate logo elements

2. **Alter typography**
   - Do not change fonts, weights, or spacing
   - Do not rotate or skew text

3. **Change colors**
   - Do not use unauthorized color combinations
   - Do not apply gradients or effects

4. **Distort proportions**
   - Do not stretch or compress the logo
   - Maintain aspect ratio when scaling

5. **Add effects**
   - No drop shadows, glows, or outlines
   - No gradients or textures

6. **Insufficient contrast**
   - Don't place logo on busy backgrounds
   - Avoid low contrast combinations

## Reusable Design Elements

The ICTAM logo contains powerful graphic elements that can be extracted and reused to create consistent branded materials. These elements help extend the visual identity across various applications.

### 1. Circle Patterns

#### The Connected Circles System
The logo features circles of varying sizes connected by nodes, creating a dynamic network pattern.

**Sizes Available:**
- Large circles (central hub design)
- Medium circles
- Small circles (nodes)
- Ring circles (hollow center)

**How to Reuse:**
- **Background patterns**: Create scattered circle patterns on backgrounds
- **Section dividers**: Use single large circles as decorative elements
- **Corner accents**: Place circle clusters in corners of designs
- **Headers/Footers**: Create horizontal bands with circle patterns
- **Overlays**: Layer semi-transparent circles for depth
- **Icons**: Use individual circles as bullet points or list markers

#### Circle Pattern Applications:

**1. Hero Sections**
```
- Place large circles (red or white) in top-right corner
- Add smaller connected circles cascading down
- Creates dynamic, modern look
- Use 20-30% opacity for subtlety
```

**2. Card Backgrounds**
```
- Add 2-3 circles of varying sizes in corners
- Use gray (#585a5c) at 5-10% opacity
- Creates visual interest without overwhelming content
```

**3. Section Transitions**
```
- Create horizontal divider with circle pattern
- Alternate red and white circles
- Add connecting lines between them
```

**4. Event Banners**
```
- Large circle on right edge (partially visible)
- Medium circles scattered on left
- Small node circles connecting them
- See expo booth designs in guide (page 22)
```

### 2. The Map Shape

#### Abstract Malawi Silhouette
The vertical elongated shape represents Malawi's geographic outline.

**How to Reuse:**
- **Watermarks**: Light gray silhouette as page background
- **Frames**: Use as border or frame element
- **Infographics**: Container for statistics or information
- **Navigation**: Vertical sidebar design element
- **Social media**: Profile picture frame or story template

### 3. Connection Lines

#### Network Lines and Nodes
The thin lines connecting circles represent digital connectivity.

**How to Reuse:**
- **Timelines**: Connect events or milestones
- **Process flows**: Show steps in a procedure
- **Organizational charts**: Connect team members or departments
- **Animated elements**: For web/digital presentations
- **Borders**: Create decorative borders with node patterns

### 4. Complete Patterns

#### Full Network Pattern
The complete interconnected system of circles, nodes, and lines.

**How to Reuse:**
- **Hero backgrounds**: Large-scale pattern with opacity
- **Texture overlays**: Subtle background texture
- **Print materials**: Letterheads, business cards
- **Digital backgrounds**: Website sections, slides
- **Merchandise**: T-shirts, banners, flags (see pages 18-21)

## Design System Integration

### Spacing with Circle Elements
- Use circle diameters as spacing units
- Small circle (40px) = 1 unit
- Medium circle (100px) = 2.5 units
- Large circle (200px) = 5 units

### Layering
1. **Background layer**: Large, low-opacity circles
2. **Content layer**: Text and main content
3. **Accent layer**: Small, higher-opacity circles
4. **Interactive layer**: Hover effects and animations

### Animation Suggestions
- **Pulse effect**: Scale circles 100% to 110% on loop
- **Float effect**: Gentle up/down movement
- **Fade in**: Stagger opacity from 0 to final value
- **Connect**: Animate lines drawing between circles

## Best Practices Summary

### DO:
✓ Use official logo files from brand package
✓ Maintain minimum clear space
✓ Respect minimum size requirements
✓ Use approved color combinations
✓ Use circle elements to extend brand identity
✓ Maintain consistent spacing and proportions

✗ Place logo on busy backgrounds
